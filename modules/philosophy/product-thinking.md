# Product Thinking

## Build Around The User's Next Step

Features should make the next useful action clearer.

Operational rule: describe every feature by the user problem it solves.

## Prefer Small Useful Releases

A small shipped improvement teaches more than a large hidden plan.

Operational rule: define the smallest valuable version before expanding scope.

## Make Constraints Visible

Constraints are not excuses; they are design inputs.

Operational rule: write down time, team, platform, and product constraints before making large tradeoffs.

## Maintainability Is Product Work

Users feel maintenance quality through reliability, speed, and support.

Operational rule: include maintenance and documentation in project planning, not as cleanup afterthoughts.

## Good Projects Onboard Fast

The faster a contributor understands the project, the more resilient the project becomes.

Operational rule: README, setup, architecture, and current state should answer the first hour of questions.
